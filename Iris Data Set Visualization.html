Out[10]:
<AxesSubplot:xlabel='sepal_width', ylabel='petal_width'>
Box plots are used to represent statistical data, making it easy to identify outliers. This plot is drawn sepal width against petal width, illustrating the min and max petal widths with respect to their sepal widths. For instance, species having a sepal width of 3.1 have a minimum petal width of about 0.1, and a maximum of about 2.4. 25% lies at around 0.2, 50% at around 1.5 and 75% at 2.0, with no outliers. Sepal width of 3.4 has 3 outliers.
